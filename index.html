How it works

Set it once.
Forget it exists.

Ibis lives in your system tray and watches a folder you choose. Every audio file you drop in becomes a transcript, automatically, while you do other things.

1

Point Ibis at a folder

Choose a Watch Folder during setup. Ibis monitors it silently in the background, with no window open and no manual intervention required.

2

Drop in an audio file

Add any MP3, WAV, or M4A file. Ibis detects it immediately and begins transcribing using the on-device AI model — no internet, no waiting for a server.

3

Your transcript is ready

A .txt file appears in your Transcripts folder, timestamped and named to match your recording. A toast notification confirms it’s done. The original audio moves to Completed.

Privacy, by design

Your audio is sensitive.
Treat it that way.

Cloud transcription services receive your recordings, process them on remote servers, and store them under terms of service you agreed to but probably didn’t read. Ibis doesn’t. The AI model runs on your machine. Your audio never moves.

No audio ever leaves your machine

Ibis uses a fully local AI model. No internet connection is required after installation — ever.
